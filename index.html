Leadership on the pitch

3 Years of Football — NSG, Leadership & Growth

From C Division co-captain to starting centre-back, my football journey reflects consistent commitment, tactical growth, and the ability to lead under pressure — skills I carry directly into business.

2024 — C DIVISION
Co-Captain · NSG Top 8 · Exco Member · Bangkok Trip
In my first year of NSG competition, I took on the co-captain role for the C Division squad — a responsibility I held not just in title, but in practice. Leading warm-ups, communicating game plans with coaches, and keeping team morale high during tough fixtures were all part of the job. We pushed the team to a Top 8 finish in NSG, competing against schools from across Singapore. I also represented the school on an overseas football trip to Bangkok, Thailand — an experience that strengthened team bonds and broadened my perspective beyond the classroom.
Co-Captain NSG Top 8 Bangkok 2024 Exco
2025 — B DIVISION
NSG Top 8 · Exco Member · Bangkok Trip
Stepping up to B Division meant facing older, more experienced opponents — and yet the team delivered another Top 8 NSG result. My role evolved from frontline leader to a more supportive, organising presence as I continued in the Exco, helping manage CCA operations, coordinate training schedules, and mentor younger players entering the setup. A second Bangkok trip followed, cementing my place as one of the team's experienced hands abroad.
NSG Top 8 Bangkok 2025 Exco B Division
2026 — PRESENT
Starting Centre-Back · NSG Participant · Exco Member
In my final year, I earned the starting centre-back position — one of the most demanding roles on the pitch. Centre-backs must read the game before it happens, communicate constantly with their goalkeeper and midfielders, and make decisive calls under pressure. It is, in many ways, a business role: manage risk, organise people, and set the tone for how the team performs. Three years of NSG. Three years of Exco. One consistent thread: showing up, stepping up, and delivering.

My motivation

Why Business at Temasek Poly?

Business stood out to me precisely because it brings together the things I am naturally drawn to: leading people, thinking through problems, and finding the best path forward under pressure. As team captain, I did not just play the game — I read it, planned for it, and brought my teammates along with me. That experience taught me that leadership is not about authority; it is about communication, trust, and decisive action.

Temasek Polytechnic's School of Business is where I want to take these instincts further. The ApLM programme I attended gave me a first look at what TP offers — and it confirmed that this is the right environment for me. I am not applying because I want a qualification. I am applying because I want to learn how to build things, lead teams, and contribute meaningfully to the business world — and I believe TP is the place that will help me do that.
